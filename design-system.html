Principles

Editorial wellness
Not a fitness tracker. Not a clinical app. NutriFlow reads like a thoughtful magazine — calm typography, ample whitespace, numbers as headlines, illustration as restraint.
Data over decoration
Calorie counts and macro values are the story. We use Fraunces (serif display) to give numbers weight and Nunito (clean sans) to keep supporting text quiet. No food photography — illustrations are minimal, icons stand in for visuals.
Thoughtful friend, not coach
We never lecture, shame, or gamify. No streaks, no badges, no aggressive notifications. Maria gets information; she decides what to do with it.
Mobile-first interaction
Designed for thumbs. Touch targets ≥44px. No hover states on mobile — :active tap feedback (scale 0.96, 80ms) communicates response. Hover rules wrapped in @media (hover: hover) for desktop preview only.
System discipline
Pills for interactive controls (search, steppers, browse buttons). Rectangles for input fields (dish name, ingredient rows). Sage for system, coral for emotional accent (Fat macro, calorie flame), amber for Carbs, terracotta for dinner. Every color choice is intentional.

09

Voice & tone

We sound like a thoughtful friend — not a coach, not a clinician. Warm, direct, and never preachy. Numbers fade into the background; the person stays in focus.
"I just want to know if this meal fits — not fill out a form."
— Maria, 32, marketing manager
Do write
"Add to today" — clear, present
"160 kcal remaining today" — factual
"Good evening, Alex" — warm but not familiar
"Match your remaining kcal" — helpful
Don't write
"Crush your goals today!" — coach voice
"You're 8% over your target!" — alarmist
"Hey buddy, ready to track?" — overfamiliar
"Don't break your streak!" — gamified pressure
10

Accessibility

Touch targets
Minimum 44×44px tap area for all interactive elements. Buttons that look smaller (like the 36×36 quantity stepper) include invisible padding to meet the threshold. Verified on all primary actions, secondary actions, list rows, and tab bar items.
Color contrast
All text–background combinations meet WCAG AA standards:
  • Primary text #2D2D2D on cream #FAFAF5 — ratio ~13.4 (AAA)
  • Secondary text #8B8B8B on cream — ratio ~3.5 (AA Large)
  • Primary green #7BAE7F on cream — ratio ~3.1 (AA Large; headings/buttons only)
  • White text on green button — ratio ~3.2 (AA Large)
Motion & mobility
All transitions are under 200ms (fade 150ms, tap 80ms). No parallax, no auto-rotation, no autoplay. Future enhancement: respect prefers-reduced-motion to disable transitions entirely for users with vestibular sensitivities.
